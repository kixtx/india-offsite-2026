FAQs

Common questions about the Bangkok offsite

Do I need a visa to enter Thailand? ▼
Indian nationals can enter Thailand visa-free for up to 30 days under the 2024 policy update. Make sure your passport is valid for at least 6 months from April 27, 2026.
What is the TDAC form and do I need to fill it? ▼
Yes — the Thailand Digital Arrival Card (TDAC) is mandatory for all visitors. Fill it at tdac.immigration.go.th after booking your flight. Details must match your passport exactly. Download or print your confirmation for arrival.
How do I book my flights? ▼
Book your own flights — outbound from any Indian city to Bangkok, return from Bangkok on April 30. Recommended: Air India Express IX 882 (10:00 AM IST → ~4:00 PM BKK). Budget: INR 30,000–31,000 round trip. Reimbursements processed in batch around April 20.
Who coordinates airport pickup? ▼
Airport pickup is arranged by Kirti. Share your exact arrival time and terminal with her on Slack so she can coordinate the group transfer from Suvarnabhumi.
What are the room arrangements? ▼
Rooms are twin-sharing. Roommate preferences were collected in advance. Reach out to Kirti on Slack if you have any questions about your room assignment.
What should I wear to the Pinnacle Awards ceremony? ▼
The Pinnacle Awards is a celebratory evening. Smart-formal or semi-formal attire is recommended — button-down shirts, blazers, or similar. More details will be shared closer to the date.
What is the weather like in Bangkok in April? ▼
Very hot and humid — expect 33–38°C (91–100°F). Pack light, breathable clothing and sunscreen. Meeting rooms will be air-conditioned, so a light layer is handy.
What currency should I carry? ▼
Thai Baht (THB). Exchange at the airport or use an ATM. Credit cards are widely accepted at hotels, malls, and restaurants. Keep some cash for street food and markets.
Will there be vegetarian / dietary options? ▼
Yes — Thai cuisine is very accommodating. Please share any dietary restrictions or allergies with Kirti or Varsha in advance.
What is the Bangkok Miles pin? ▼
The Bangkok Miles pin is an exclusive commemorative badge given only to offsite attendees — a keepsake of our time in Thailand. Separate anniversary pins will also be distributed; Kirti and Varsha will bring extras back for India team members who couldn’t attend.
